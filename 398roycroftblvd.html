Negotiation Strategy

Overall Position

The property's premium location is offset by severe, documented defects in safety and habitability. The seller's position is weakened by material facts that must be disclosed to any potential buyer. The negotiation should focus on a price adjustment reflecting the significant, immediate costs required to make the home safe and compliant.

High-Impact Negotiation Points

  • Uninhabitable Heating System & Asbestos Hazard: Boiler is non-functional, requiring abatement and replacement. (Ask: ~$15,000 credit or pre-closing repair)
  • Systemic Electrical Hazards: Dangerous sub-panel, no grounding, missing GFCIs. (Ask: ~$6,000 credit or pre-closing repair)
  • Illegal & Unsafe Attic Living Space: No egress window on 3rd floor is a life-safety violation. (Ask: ~$4,000 credit or pre-closing repair)
  • Compromised Building Envelope: Deteriorated chimney and masonry allowing water intrusion. (Ask: ~$4,500 credit or pre-closing repair)

Recommended Strategy: Seller-Funded Repairs

Request seller to complete critical repairs pre-closing using licensed contractors. This transfers financial risk and ensures you take possession of a safe, compliant home. A starting request for a credit of $29,500 is a well-supported alternative.
